MacroBox v2.0

All files available on GitHub.

Overview

Things are simple.
The MacroBox v1 simply is designed in a non-functional manner. On top of that, it’s far too expensive. That is why I made the cheap and dirty MacroBox v2. It is nothing special but a simple Arduino Micro wired to some Cherry MX Bue Switches.

I was planning to make a MacroBox Pro. That would have consisted of a PCB with an Atmega32u4, RGB LEDs, and a custom-made GUI to program the switches. Unfortunately, I realized how many MacroPads are already in existence and that the world simply doesn’t need another.


Printed Circuit Board

The MacroBox v2 is manually wired to save on cost.
The schematic can be found on EasyEDA.

The circuit consists of an Arduino Micro, fourteen white 3mm LEDs, fourteen 100ohm resistors, and ten Cherry MX Blue Switches. Yes, a resistor for each LED, it is in fact overkill.

The LEDs are wired in parallel. The LEDs are connected straight to the Arduino’s GND and 5V pins. Instead of using a matrix, the four LEDs displaying the current mode are each connected to their own digital pin.

Lastly, the Cherry MX Switches are also connected to their own pin. Why? There are enough pins. I ain’t gotta be fancy.
